The solution

Envelope — money in, salaries out,
policy in between. Nothing leaked.

01 · Money in

Customers pay from any chain — Base, Arbitrum, BNB, Solana — or a card, UPI, SEPA. It all settles as USDC in one treasury you control.

02 · Policy

Salary bands & caps live encrypted before they touch a database. An on-chain program enforces the monthly cap and requires co-signers — nobody reads a salary to approve a run.

03 · Salaries out

One approval pays every contractor in a single shielded batch. The public ledger sees one entry — no names, no amounts. Each recipient gets a one-time stealth claim link, greeted by their .sol handle.

The part Stripe and Wise can’t ship: a treasury with no single private key, salary figures that never sit in plaintext, and payroll that settles on a public chain without disclosing a thing.

Envelope04 / 12

One idea: Envelope is one product that does the whole loop, and the loop is private by construction — not "we added a privacy mode". Walk left to right: in, policy, out. The encrypted-policy bit is the surprising one — emphasise that an approver never has to see a number to approve the run.

"Why not just use a privacy mixer for the payouts?" — Mixers protect one transaction; they don't give you a programmable treasury, encrypted comp policy, on-chain spending caps, or recipient claim links. Envelope is the whole workflow a finance team needs, with privacy as the substrate — not a bolt-on you remember to use.
